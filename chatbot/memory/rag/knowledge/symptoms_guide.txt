Symptom Guide for Diabetes Patients — Dizziness and Blood Sugar

Postural Hypotension (Dizziness When Standing Up)

Trigger: Standing up or changing position quickly, especially after meals
Symptoms: Dizziness or lightheadedness when standing up, improves quickly when sitting or lying back down; brief vision darkening; NO hand trembling, NO cold sweat
Cause: After eating, blood flow shifts to the digestive system, temporarily reducing blood supply to the brain. This is NOT related to blood sugar levels.
What to do:
- Stand up slowly — sit on the edge of the bed or chair for 30 seconds before fully standing
- Drink plenty of water and stay well-hydrated
- Do NOT eat food — this is NOT low blood sugar, eating will not help
- Avoid standing for long periods or standing in the heat
See a doctor if: Dizziness happens frequently, or is accompanied by chest pain or fainting

Low Blood Sugar / Hypoglycaemia (blood glucose below 3.9 mmol/L)

Trigger: Skipping meals, before meals, after exercise, or when medication dose is too high
Symptoms: Shaking hands, cold sweat, rapid heartbeat, weakness, hunger, irritability; does NOT improve when sitting down
What to do: Immediately eat 15g of fast-acting carbohydrates (glucose tablet, fruit juice, honey), check blood sugar again after 15 minutes

Key difference — dizzy after standing up vs low blood sugar:

Dizzy after standing up from a meal → Postural hypotension → Stand up slowly + drink water. Do NOT eat snacks or food. Eating will NOT help.
Dizzy with shaking hands + hunger when fasting or after exercise → Low blood sugar → Eat something sugary immediately.

If a patient says "I stood up after dinner and felt dizzy, is my blood sugar low?" — this is most likely postural hypotension, NOT hypoglycaemia. Advise them to sit back down, drink water, and stand up slowly next time. Do not suggest eating.

High Blood Sugar After Meals

Symptoms: Tiredness, thirst, frequent urination, blurry vision — usually appears 1–2 hours after eating
Cause: Blood sugar spike above 10 mmol/L
Management: A 15–20 minute walk after meals helps reduce post-meal blood sugar peak

糖尿病患者常见症状鉴别指南

体位性低血压（餐后头晕）

触发：站起来或改变体位时，尤其是饭后
症状：站起时头晕，坐下或躺下后迅速好转；眼前短暂发黑；无手抖、无冷汗
原因：饭后血液集中到消化系统，导致脑部供血短暂减少；与血糖水平无直接关系
处理方法：
- 起身时放慢速度，先坐在床边或椅边停留30秒再站起
- 多喝水，保持充足水分
- 不需要额外进食——这不是低血糖，吃东西无法改善
- 避免长时间站立或在烈日下站立
就医指征：头晕频繁发作、伴有胸痛或晕厥

低血糖（血糖低于3.9 mmol/L）

触发：空腹、餐前、运动后、服药剂量偏大时
症状：手抖、出冷汗、心跳加快、全身无力、饥饿感、情绪烦躁；坐下后不会明显好转
处理方法：立即进食15克快速吸收碳水（葡萄糖片、果汁、蜂蜜），15分钟后测血糖

关键区别

饭后站起头晕 → 体位性低血压 → 慢慢起身 + 喝水，不需要吃东西
空腹/运动后头晕+手抖 → 低血糖 → 立即补充含糖食物

餐后血糖偏高引起的不适

症状：疲倦、口渴、尿频、视力模糊——通常在餐后1-2小时出现
原因：血糖升高（>10 mmol/L）
处理：餐后散步15-20分钟有助于降低餐后血糖峰值
