Obesity increases the risk of many other health conditions, and it is
one of the major causes of health disorders that is preventable.
Obesity in Singapore:
The prevalence of obesity in Singapore increased from 10.5 percent
in 2019-2020 to 11.6 percent in 2021-2022. This increase was mainly
observed among those aged 18 to 29, and 40 to 59.
Obesity in Singapore
Obesity is most commonly caused by a combination of excessive food
energy intake, lack of physical activity and genetic susceptibility.

Identify and improve your daily sitting habits. Reduce your sitting
time and increase your walking to burn more calories and boost your
fitness level!

Obesity can be managed conservatively by lifestyle modification such as:
•	 Diet modification (energy input)
Examples: small frequent meals, low calorie diet, etc
• 	Physical activity or volitional exercise (energy output)
Examples: aerobic, resistance, flexibility exercises
• 	NEAT (Non-Exercise Activity Thermogenesis) which is energy
expenditure associated with spontaneous, non-exercise
physical activity.
Examples: housework, cooking, climbing stairs, gardening, walking
instead of taking a bus and parking a short distance away from your
destination.

Aerobic exercise is a moderately intense structured physical activity,
which improves your cardio-respiratory fitness and is good for weight
loss as it increases your energy expenditure and burns more calories.

Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) is a significant contributor to total daily
energy expenditure. Aging results in loss of both muscle mass and the
metabolic quality of skeletal muscles, resulting in reduced BMR.
Resistance training increases Fat Free Mass, which is the major
determinant (50–75%) of the resting metabolism to contribute to
improved BMR and improve metabolism.

PATIENTS
-	 Choose low-impact activities
like cycling or swimming
if you have joint pain or

-	 Observe RICE ( Rest, Ice,

-	 Resume safe exercises 1-2

FOR DIABETIC PATIENTS
-	 Check blood sugar before and
after exercise
- 	 Don't exercise if blood sugar
is >16.7mmol/l
- 	 Eat a snack if blood sugar is
<5.5mmol/l
- 	 Keep emergency
carbohydrates handy
(e.g. sweets/fruit juice)
- 	 Watch for signs of low blood
sugar: sweating, clammy
skin, dizziness, fatigue and
hunger
FOR WEIGHT LOSS
-	 Aim to lose 0.5-1 kg per week
-	 Follow your diet plan from the
dietitian
-	 Schedule exercise time or
make a weekly routine
- 	 Exercise with a buddy if
you prefer
- 	 Choose low-impact activities
like cycling or swimming for
joint pain in the legs
- 	 Keep track of your weight
loss using a weighing scale or
weight loss phone app
FOR CARDIAC PATIENTS
-	 Get doctor's approval before
starting new exercises
- 	 Watch for signs of heart
attack: chest pain,
breathlessness, cold sweats,
dizziness and nausea vomiting
- 	 Follow fluid restrictions for
heart failure as per doctor's
advice
